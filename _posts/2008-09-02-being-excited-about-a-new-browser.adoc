It's been a long time since I've been excited about a new browser. Theoretically, I've never been excited about a new browser that was announced. I remember being excited when link:http://aol.com[AOL] resurrected link:http://netscape.com[Netscape] -- but that turned into a flaming pile of poo and Netscape lost dominance being THE browser to use. Like many users at that time frame, I used Internet Explorer 5 and at the time it was best of breed, then a new challenger arose.

The link:http://mozilla.org[Mozilla]Foundation announced they were taking the open-source bits of the Netscape browser and making a new slimmer browser called Firebird. Because of issues of legal and copyright, Firebird was renamed to link:http://firefox.com[Firefox]. I've been using this browser since Firebird and I have had no reason to move to a different primary browser. I've tried link:http://flock.com[Flock] and link:http://www.apple.com/safari/[Safari], there hasn't been a sticky reason to keep using those over Firefox. I was excited, kind of, about the release of Firefox version 3. But that wasn't a new and different browser, it was more of the same.

With last night's announcement of link:http://googleblog.blogspot.com/2008/09/fresh-take-on-browser.html[Google's New Chrome Browser], they put up a nice link:http://www.google.com/googlebooks/chrome/[little]webcomic that explains the features it offers. The security, privacy, and performance enhancements alone make this a must-watch for browsers. When it is actually released later today, we'll see how I feel then.
