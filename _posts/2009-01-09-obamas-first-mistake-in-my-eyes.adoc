I could pick on a few things actually, once again I didn't vote for the guy, but I don't absolutely detest him. I'm in wait-and-see mode. link:http://arstechnica.com[Ars Technic]a is reporting about Obama's plea to link:http://arstechnica.com/news.ars/post/20090109-markey-says-congress-should-consider-delaying-dtv-day.html[push back the transition to digital TV]. If we look at the digital TV migration timeline, we can see the originally scheduled date was Dec. 31, 2006. Because of multiple issues, it was pushed back, and this brings us to this upcoming Feb. 17th as the transition time.

We can argue that the government hasn't done enough to inform the public or make it easy for the public, but I would argue otherwise. I was well aware of this migration when it was first discussed over 10 years ago. All in all the government has pushed out this transition longer than necessary. You can say that it's a good cautious thing they are doing by waiting and delaying, but every delay costs the taxpayers more money.
