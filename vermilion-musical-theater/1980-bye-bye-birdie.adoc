= 1980 -- Bye Bye Birdie

=== Bye Bye Birdie -- March 21-12, 28-29, 1980

image::/testsite/images/bybye1980.jpeg[,role="text-center"]

“Bye Bye Birdie” is a popular American musical comedy that tells the story of a rock ‘n’ roll superstar named Conrad Birdie. The plot centers around Birdie, who is drafted into the army, leaving his devoted fan base devastated. To boost his popularity and provide a farewell event, his manager concocts a plan for Birdie to give “one last kiss” to a randomly selected fan before he leaves for the military. The lucky fan chosen for this publicity stunt is a teenage girl named Kim MacAfee.

The musical explores the chaos and excitement that ensue in the small town of Sweet Apple, Ohio, as they prepare for Birdie’s arrival. Kim’s boyfriend, Hugo, is less than thrilled about her involvement with Birdie, leading to romantic entanglements and comedic misunderstandings. Throughout the story, the characters grapple with the impact of fame and the changes it brings to their lives.

“Bye Bye Birdie” is known for its catchy songs and energetic dance numbers, making it a lighthearted and entertaining musical that satirizes the phenomenon of teen idols and celebrity culture in the 1950s. The musical is often praised for its humor, memorable tunes, and colorful characters, making it a beloved classic in the world of musical theater.
